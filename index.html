Canadian Cancer Society

Redesign a RWD website prototype for the Canadian Cancer Society website

Who is the Canadian Cancer Society?

The Canadian Cancer Society is a national, community-based organization of staff and volunteers whose mission is the eradication of cancer and the enhancement of the quality of life of people living with cancer.

Overview

The Canadian Cancer Society website has been designed to help family members, friends and communities eradicate cancer and improve the quality of life of people living with Cancer.

With users dealing with the stress of being in-directly affected by cancer, how can we simplify and re-organize the website to create a smooth and easy experience?

  • My Role: UX/UI Designer
  • Type: Group Project
  • Team Members: Zine Boukebir, Maxine Yang & Margaret Shatara
  • Time: 2 Weeks
  • Tools: Figma, Adobe XD, InVision, Photoshop, Illustrator, Miro

Problem

With cancer patients, family and friends dealing with exceptionally high stress, the lack of community support found on the Canadian Cancer Society's website has proven to negatively affect the users well being by causing additional anxiety due to the lack of mental support.

Furthermore, the disorganized and text heavy website causes further confusion when navigating through the website which could potentially lead to frustrations and users exiting the page to use another resource.

Solution

Create an accessible community that allows users to feel comfortable to reach out to the Canadian Cancer Society while also providing a smooth and effortless user experience that leads to a better quality of life.

Proto-Persona

The proto-persona was created with many details allowing room to discover potential pain points during user interviews.

Competitor Analysis

A competitor analysis was conducted to get a better understand how users who are affected directly or indirectly by cancer get help from the organizations and the community and how they can contribute back to others who need help. (Competitors list below)

We discovered that some competitors have clear and distinct categories, easy navigation, quick access to contact information, when others have too much condensed information all over the screen which the user can be lost and the only way to access the contact information is to navigate to the bottom of the page.

Research Findings

We did more research to better understand the users and in particular the caregivers who are friends or family members.

  • A lot of caregivers or close family members reach out to online forums / communities for support and guidance.
  • Canadian Cancer Society has volunteers that have been directly / indirectly affected by cancer to help support those who are most vulnerable. however would it be helpful if those in need could contact them directly or a hotline for support at any time?
  • Many users prefer physically calling in or chatting online with someone they can relate to.
  • Users should be able to have access to information in regards to how to take care of themselves and access to a mental health hotline.
  • Users who are already involved / affected by cancer tend to be drawn towards donating more than those not affected.
  • Research has found that when donating , generosity appears to have especially strong associations with psychological health and well-being.
  • The menu in the Canadian cancer society page specifically for close family and friends is very small and unwelcoming. Users don't find it easy to find the support system / community set in place at the Canadian cancer society.

Usability Testing

We conducted 3 different usability testing with users to better see how their experience was and get their feedback in order to iterate our design and make it more user-friendly.
The 3 scenarios cover the donation process, how to get information related to a category of cancer and how to get help through the online community platform.

Based on users feedback, we made some iteration on our prototype which includes:

  • Adding home button to the mobile prototype
  • Adding more choices on the donation page
  • Make "Join the Online Community" button more visible on the home page
  • Reposition the Donate button for easy access

Conclusion

Re-designing a website for a medical non-profit organization is more challenging to get in touch with the stakeholders on the current situation COVID-19. We'll keep trying to contact the organization to better understand their objectives and get their feedback.

With our redesigned website, we think that our users who are mainly the caregivers will find the help they need from the community and that will reduce their stress which can affect their friends or family members who have cancer. Our donation redesigned pages will allow users to contribute to the society with an easy and simplistic way.

Mobile first design is the way to go when designing responsive web pages. It was much easier to expand content than to collapse it.
